The wordmark

The logo, locked.

SVG vector. Transparent background. Three locked design moves: "duffle" is acid front, slap pink mid, pitch deep. "up" mirrors — slap pink front, acid mid, pitch deep. "up" tilts -8° upward — the word does its action. Get up. Shut up. Duffle up. Works on any surface below.

CONSTRUCTION

"duffle": acid (#CCFF00) front
→ slap pink (#FF1B8D) offset behind
→ pitch (#0A0A0A) deepest
→ sits on baseline

"up": slap pink (#FF1B8D) front
→ acid (#CCFF00) offset behind
→ pitch (#0A0A0A) deepest
→ rotated -8° (rises right)

USAGE RULES

Never straighten the "up" — the tilt is the brand.
Never use on busy photographic backgrounds.
Never recolor the wordmark.
Always place on solid color, gradient, or muted overlay.
Always maintain shadow proportions when scaling.

04 — Voice

Zero apologies.

Confident. Cheeky. Direct. Slightly disrespectful of OTA conventions. Marketing surfaces lean harder. Functional UI stays cleaner but never goes generic.

Button labels

✓ DO

"Pack my duffle"

"Get me out there"

"Got a place?"

Cheeky. Active. Specific to Duffleup.

✗ DON'T

"Book Now"

"Click here!"

"Submit Application"

Generic. Salesy. Could be any OTA.

Error messages

✓ DO

"Couldn't reach our servers. Try again, or hit hello@duffleup.in."

Says what went wrong. Tells you what to do.

✗ DON'T

"Oops! Something went wrong. We apologize for any inconvenience."

Apologetic. Vague. Customer-service-script energy.

Confirmations

✓ DO

"Locked in. Pack accordingly."

"Sorted. Check your inbox."

Direct. Specific. Tells you what's next.

✗ DON'T

"Yay! Your booking has been successfully confirmed! 🎉"

Performative cheerfulness. We're not a children's app.
